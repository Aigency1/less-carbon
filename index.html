Why this matters now

The pressure is landing in inboxes, not in policy papers.

Three pressures are now converging. Together they explain why a supplier email arrived this week, and why the response window is shorter than most SMEs realise.

01

This is a three-year wave, and it has already started.

Under AASB S2, Group 1 entities have reporting periods from 1 January 2025, Group 2 from 1 July 2026 and Group 3 from 1 July 2027. Scope 3 is the emissions of their supply chain, which means yours. The only question is which group's deadline catches you.

Source · AASB S2 (2024)
02

Roughly 80% of supply-chain emissions sit with SME suppliers.

Reporting entities can't hit their Scope 3 numbers without supplier data. Procurement teams have started sending the email. The cleanest path back is a recognised Supplier Emissions Certificate.

Source · PwC Net Zero Economy Index (2024)
03

No certificate, no panel. It's credentials, not consent.

Tender shortlists, panel renewals and procurement reviews now ask for emissions data alongside insurance and safety. It's like a union site: no ticket, no work. "We're working on it" is no longer an answer.

Source · ABS supplier procurement surveys (2025)

Frequently asked

The six questions most SMEs ask first.

If yours isn't here, the methodology page goes deeper. We update this page based on what people actually ask.

What is a Supplier Emissions Certificate?

A one-page document showing your business's Scope 1 and 2 emissions for a reporting period, methodology, assumptions and a certificate reference number, prepared for use with customers, lenders and tender processes.

Do I need a carbon assessment for AASB S2?

If your business supplies a reporting entity that needs Scope 3 supply-chain data, you'll likely be asked for Scope 1 and 2 numbers. Use our Pre-Qualifier to check.

How long does an SME carbon assessment take?

7 business days from the moment your records are uploaded. One clean SLA, no rush tiers to decide between.

How much does a carbon assessment cost?

Affordable pricing, calculated online before you pay. Your price is calculated from your business profile, across six drivers (sites, fleet, fuels, refrigeration, employees, ANZSIC industry), and shown before payment. No quote process, no surprise invoices. RE bulk programs receive a 20% discount on volumes of 50+ suppliers.

What's the methodology?

We base our reporting on government and industry standards, in standardised formats your clients will accept and appreciate. Emissions are calculated under the Greenhouse Gas Protocol, recognised under AASB S2, with methodology and assumptions recorded.

Can I download a sample certificate before I buy?

Yes. Our Sample Certificate page has both the Supplier Emissions Certificate and the Carbon Assessment report to view or download directly, no email required.
