What the register shows

What a verified certificate tells you.

When you look up a certificate, the register confirms:

  • Certificate status: Valid, Expired, Replaced or Revoked
  • Issue date and expiry date
  • Supplier name
  • Scope coverage (Scope 1 + Scope 2, financial year)

The verification result doesn't expose emissions tonnage. That detail stays with the certificate holder, who can share their Assessment Report directly if needed.

A note on dates: a certificate reports emissions for the financial year assessed and remains valid for 12 months from its issue date. A certificate covering FY2025–26 data issued in May 2026 stays current until May 2027.

Register questions

Common questions.

What does a verified certificate confirm?

The register confirms certificate status (Valid, Expired, Replaced or Revoked), issue date and expiry date, supplier name, and scope coverage (Scope 1 + Scope 2 and the financial year assessed). It does not display emissions tonnage; that detail stays with the certificate holder.

How do I verify a Supplier Emissions Certificate?

Check by certificate number, search by supplier ABN, or scan the QR code on a Less Carbon certificate. While certificate volumes build, verification is completed by our team: send your request and we confirm status within one business day.

How long is a certificate valid?

12 months from its date of issue. A certificate reports emissions for the financial year assessed and remains current for 12 months from issue, so a certificate covering FY2025–26 data issued in May 2026 stays valid until May 2027.

What if a supplier isn't on the register?

It means Less Carbon has not issued them a current Supplier Emissions Certificate. Reporting entities can scope a supplier program to have suppliers assessed, and suppliers can start their own assessment online, from $995 + GST.
