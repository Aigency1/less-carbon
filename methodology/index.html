Think of it like a tax assessment.

A tax assessment is only as strong as the process behind it: real records from the business, a standardised government method, and an output that can be audited line by line. That is exactly how a Less Carbon assessment works.

We take your actual records, energy bills, fuel logs, fleet data, operational documents. Not estimates, not guesses. We apply the GHG Protocol method using the Australian Government's published NGA Factors. And we put the result in a standardised, auditable format that any reporting entity, accountant or auditor can read the same way, every time.

Where the same source data, assumptions and factor version are used, the same calculation rules produce the same result. The method is controlled, the factors are published, and any judgement or assumption is documented.

The standards behind every number

There is no single mandated certification standard for supplier emissions in Australia. So we built our methodology on the two pillars the entire reporting system already relies on, then went one level deeper.

📐

GHG Protocol Corporate Standard

The international method for scoping and calculating Scope 1 and Scope 2 emissions. It is the framework the Australian reporting regime itself is built on.

🇦🇺

National Greenhouse Accounts factors

The Australian Government's published emission factors for fuels, electricity and energy. Updates are applied through our calculation engine automatically, and the factor version used is recorded for traceability.

🔬

ANZSIC line-by-line depth

Each assessment is mapped to the relevant ANZSIC industry classification and calculated line by line by emissions source, rather than produced as a single top-level estimate. That level of detail helps make the output easier to review, compare and support.

How your assessment is built

A controlled workflow from first form to final certificate. Here is what it means for you.

1

Structured intake

A structured online form captures your business profile, sites, fleet, fuels and operations. Nothing downstream has to guess what you meant.

2

Evidence upload

You upload source documents (bills, logs, statements) into a controlled process that builds your evidence trail from day one.

3

AI classification & extraction

Our engine reads each document, classifies it and extracts the operating data into a standard working structure.

4

Deterministic calculation

A rules engine normalises units and calculates your Scope 1 and 2 emissions using the GHG Protocol method and current NGA factors. Rules, not interpretation.

5

Quality gate

Automated checks test whether inputs are sufficient and results consistent. Gaps are flagged and chased, never papered over.

6

Human review

A qualified reviewer checks every assessment before anything leaves the building.

7

Your approval

You receive the draft certificate and assessment for review before final issue.

8

Issue & register

Your Supplier Emissions Certificate and Carbon Assessment are issued, dated and valid for 12 months.

Human eyes on every certificate

Automation does the heavy lifting. It does not get the final say.

Every certificate is reviewed by our qualified team before issue. Our reviewers hold formal training in emissions assessment and climate-related financial risk, with backgrounds in licensed and regulated industries and specialist coursework specific to the AASB S2 reporting framework.

As our benchmarking dataset grows, assessments that sit outside expected ranges for their industry and size will always be escalated to a manual review. But the standing rule today is simple: nothing is issued without human eyes on it.

What your certificate is (and what it is not)

Plain words matter here, so we will be precise.

Your certificate IS

An independent assessment of your Scope 1 and Scope 2 emissions, calculated by Less Carbon from your source evidence using the government framework, in a standardised, auditable format.

Any required party (reporting entity, auditor or regulator) can use it as supplier-provided data for their Scope 3 reporting.

Your certificate is NOT

A pass or fail. There is no threshold to clear and no grade. The number is the number.

It reports your emissions; it does not judge them.

We do not describe our certificates as audited or formally assured, because that means something specific in financial reporting and we will not borrow language we have not earned. What we offer is something the market currently lacks: a standardised, evidence-based assessment that every party in the chain can read the same way.

Data gaps

When your data has gaps

Real businesses have messy records. Our process is built for that. If the quality gate finds missing or inconsistent data, we ask you for it, specifically and clearly. If the data genuinely cannot be obtained, we either mark the line as unavailable or use a documented estimate only where the methodology permits it.

Any estimate, proxy or industry-average input is disclosed on the certificate itself, flagged line by line. Nothing is filled in quietly. Your certificate states exactly what it is built on, which is precisely why it can be trusted.

Validity

Valid for 12 months. From your date, not the government's.

Every certificate is dated and valid for 12 months from issue. Not pro-rated to a financial year, not tied to an arbitrary deadline. Your 12 months is your 12 months.

For reporting entities running supplier programs: suppliers can be scheduled through assessment in waves, and every one holds a certificate with full 12-month currency from their own issue date.

Data security & consent

Your data. Your call.

You upload commercially sensitive documents to us, so the rules are strict and simple:

  • Your full assessment and certificate go to you, and only you.
  • Anyone holding your certificate number or QR code can confirm it is current: issue date, validity and status. That is all they see.
  • If a reporting entity wants more detail, the request comes to you for approval first. One click to approve, one click to decline. Nothing is shared without your consent.
  • You set your sharing preferences at intake, and you can change them at any time.

Methodology questions, answered

What standard does Less Carbon calculate against?

The GHG Protocol Corporate Standard method, applied with the Australian Government's current published National Greenhouse Accounts (NGA) factors, broken down by ANZSIC industry classification.

Is my certificate checked by a human?

Yes. Every certificate is reviewed by our qualified team before issue, and you approve the draft before it is finalised.

Can a reporting entity use my certificate for Scope 3 reporting?

Yes. Your certificate is an independent assessment based on your source evidence, in a standardised format designed to be used as supplier-provided data in Scope 3 reporting.

Is the certificate a pass or fail?

No. There is no threshold and no grade. The certificate reports your Scope 1 and 2 emissions; it does not judge them.

What if I cannot find some of my documents?

We will ask you for the specific items first. Where data genuinely cannot be obtained, the framework permits documented industry-average figures in initial reporting periods, and any use of them is disclosed on your certificate.

How long is my certificate valid?

12 months from the date of issue.

Who can see my data?

You receive the full assessment and certificate. Third parties holding your certificate number can confirm only that it is current. Anything more requires your explicit approval, every time.

Does the assessment cover Scope 3?

Our launch service assesses Scope 1 and Scope 2, which is commonly what reporting entities ask suppliers to provide as supplier-specific emissions evidence. Scope 3 commentary and reduction pathways are on our roadmap. New to the terminology? Read our plain-English AASB S2 guide.
